The Journey

Trek Overview & Route

The Annapurna Circuit trek is globally renowned as one of the most diverse and thrilling trekking loops. The trail follows the Marsyangdi River valley into the high-altitude trans-Himalayan region of Manang, where villages steeped in Tibetan-influenced Buddhist culture await — along with a side trip to pristine Tilicho Lake (4,920m), one of the highest lakes in the world. The ultimate challenge is crossing the formidable Thorong La Pass (5,416m), before descending to the sacred pilgrimage site of Muktinath and the windswept Kali Gandaki valley.

  • Route: Chame → Manang → Tilicho Lake → Thorong La (5,416m) → Muktinath → Pokhara
  • Acclimatization: Ice Lake hike (4,600m) from Manang before the high crossing
  • Difficulty: hard — the most diverse landscapes of any Nepal trek, earned at altitude
Trek Highlights

Three Signature Circuit Experiences

From subtropical river valleys to a frozen 5,416-meter pass — no other trek in Nepal packs this much variety into fourteen days.

5,416 m

Thorong La Pass

The legendary high point of the circuit — a pre-dawn push to one of the world's great trekking passes.

  • Prayer flags and glacier panoramas at the summit
  • Sweeping views over the Kali Gandaki — Earth's deepest gorge
  • A descent into the sacred valley of Muktinath
4,920 m

Tilicho Lake

A dedicated three-day side trip to the deep-blue waters of one of the highest lakes on the planet.

  • Turquoise water ringed by glaciers and Tilicho Peak
  • Dramatic high-desert trails via Khangsar and Siri Kharka
  • Far fewer trekkers than the main circuit trail
Sacred Valleys

Manang & Muktinath

Tibetan-style stone villages and chortens on one side of the pass — an ancient pilgrimage temple on the other.

  • Manang's medieval lanes beneath Gangapurna glacier
  • Muktinath — holy to Hindus and Buddhists alike
  • The dramatic curved rock wall of Bhratang, "Heaven's Gate"
Day by Day

Your 14-Day Circuit Itinerary

A full loop of the Annapurna massif with the Tilicho Lake side trip built in — acclimatized, unhurried and unforgettable.

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400m)

Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be met and transferred to your hotel in Thamel. The rest of the day is spent on trek preparation, equipment checks, permit processing and a welcome briefing with your guide.

Day 2

Drive to Chame (2,670m) via Besisahar

A long but scenic drive takes us from Kathmandu through Besisahar and along the Marsyangdi River valley to Chame, the administrative headquarters of Manang district. The road passes waterfalls, terraced hillsides and lush green valleys.

10-11 hrs 238 km
Day 3

Trek to Lower Pisang (3,200m)

We pass through pine forests and walk under the dramatic curved rock face of Bhratang, often called Heaven's Gate. We then cross a suspension bridge to reach Lower Pisang, with splendid views of the Annapurna range.

4-5 hrs 9 km
Day 4

Trek to Manang (3,540m)

Trekkers can choose between the lower route or the upper route via Ghyaru and Ngawal — more challenging but with spectacular panoramas of Annapurna II, III and Gangapurna. Manang welcomes us with traditional Tibetan-style stone houses and chortens.

6-7 hrs 15 km
Day 5

Acclimatization — Ice Lake Hike (4,600m)

A rest and acclimatization day in Manang before the high pass. We hike up to Ice Lake for superb panoramic views of Gangapurna, Annapurna III and the surrounding glaciers, then return to Manang. You can also visit the HRA altitude clinic.

6-8 hrs 16 km

Climb high, sleep low — today's hike primes your body for Thorong La later in the week.

Day 6

Trek to Siri Kharka (4,000m)

Branching off the main circuit toward Tilicho Lake, we head west through Khangsar village and climb to Siri Kharka — a scenic resting point with open views of the Tilicho massif and surrounding high terrain.

4-5 hrs 9 km
Day 7

Trek to Tilicho Base Camp (4,150m)

We continue through the dry, landslide-prone area to reach Tilicho Base Camp, situated at the base of the massive Tilicho Peak. We rest early tonight to prepare for the high-altitude lake hike tomorrow.

3-4 hrs 6 km
Day 8

Tilicho Lake (4,920m) & Return to Siri Kharka

An early morning climb takes us to spectacular, pristine Tilicho Lake — one of the highest lakes in the world, its deep blue waters ringed by glaciers and Tilicho Peak. After time at the lake, we descend back to Siri Kharka for the night.

6-7 hrs 12 km
Day 9

Trek to Yak Kharka (4,050m)

We rejoin the main Annapurna Circuit route and climb gradually to Yak Kharka, a high-altitude grazing area. The landscape here is stark and beautiful, with views of the Chulu peaks and the upper Marsyangdi valley.

4-5 hrs 10 km
Day 10

Trek to Thorong Phedi (4,525m)

We make a slow, steady climb to Thorong Phedi, crossing a suspension bridge and passing through dramatic highland terrain. We rest at the tea houses at the foot of the pass to prepare for tomorrow's long crossing.

4-5 hrs 6.5 km
Day 11

Cross Thorong La Pass (5,416m) & Descend to Muktinath (3,760m)

The most challenging and rewarding day. We start around 3:00 AM, climbing steadily for several hours to the summit of Thorong La Pass. After celebrating at the prayer-flag-draped top, we make the long, steep descent to the sacred pilgrimage site of Muktinath.

9-10 hrs 16 km

Temperatures at the pass can drop to -10°C — windproof outerwear, heavy thermals and winter gloves are essential.

Day 12

Muktinath Temple & Drive to Pokhara (800m)

We visit the sacred Muktinath Temple in the morning before heading down to Jomsom by jeep. From Jomsom, a scenic drive along the windswept Kali Gandaki valley brings us to Pokhara for a comfortable evening by the lake.

8-9 hrs drive 183 km
Day 13

Fly Pokhara to Kathmandu

A scenic 25-minute flight takes us from Pokhara back to Kathmandu. In the evening, we gather for a farewell dinner in Thamel featuring traditional Nepalese cuisine.

Day 14

Final Departure

Transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport according to your flight schedule. Depart with unforgettable memories of the Annapurna massif.
