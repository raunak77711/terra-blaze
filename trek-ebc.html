The Journey

Trek Overview & Route

The Everest Base Camp trek is one of the most iconic and rewarding adventure journeys on Earth. This epic trek leads you into the heart of the Khumbu region, bringing you face-to-face with the world's highest peak, Mt. Everest (8,848.86m). After a thrilling flight from Kathmandu to Lukla, the trail follows the Dudh Koshi River upstream to the bustling Sherpa capital of Namche Bazaar (3,440m). Passing Tengboche Monastery, the scenic Dingboche valley and the Khumbu Glacier, we reach Everest Base Camp (5,364m) — before a sunrise climb up Kala Patthar (5,550m) delivers legendary golden views of Everest, Lhotse and Nuptse.

  • Route: Lukla → Namche Bazaar → Tengboche → EBC (5,364m) → Kala Patthar (5,550m)
  • Acclimatization: two rest days built in at Namche Bazaar and Dingboche
  • Difficulty: hard — 5-8 hours of walking per day; no technical climbing required

Day by Day

Your 14-Day Everest Itinerary

A carefully paced ascent with two full acclimatization days — engineered so your body adapts while your eyes never leave the mountains.

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400m)

Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be met and transferred to your hotel in Thamel. The rest of the day is spent on trek preparation, equipment checks, permit processing and a welcome briefing with your guide.

Day 2

Fly to Lukla (2,840m) & Trek to Phakding (2,610m)

An early morning scenic 35-minute flight brings us to the famous Tenzing-Hillary Airport at Lukla. We meet our crew and make a gentle descent along the Dudh Koshi River to Phakding for our first night on the trail.

3-4 hrs 8 km

Lukla flights are weather-dependent — keep 1-2 buffer days in Kathmandu before your international departure.

Day 3

Trek to Namche Bazaar (3,440m)

The trail crosses several suspension bridges over the Dudh Koshi, including the famous Hillary Suspension Bridge. After registering permits at Sagarmatha National Park in Monjo, we make a steep climb to the Sherpa trade hub of Namche Bazaar.

6-7 hrs 10.5 km
Day 4

Acclimatization Day in Namche Bazaar

To adjust to the altitude, we spend a full day in Namche. We hike up to the Everest View Hotel (3,880m) for our first stunning panorama of Everest, Lhotse, Ama Dablam and Thamserku before returning to Namche for the night.

3-4 hrs
Day 5

Trek to Tengboche (3,860m)

Following a scenic hillside path, we descend to Phunki Thenga for lunch, then climb through rhododendron and pine forests to Tengboche — home to the largest and most sacred monastery in the Khumbu region.

5-6 hrs 10 km
Day 6

Trek to Dingboche (4,410m)

We descend through birch and rhododendron forests, cross the Imja Khola and walk up to Pangboche village. The trail then transitions into a treeless alpine landscape as we enter the beautiful Dingboche valley.

5-6 hrs 11 km
Day 7

Acclimatization Day in Dingboche

Now above 4,000 meters, a second acclimatization day is crucial. We take a scenic hike for sweeping views of Makalu, Lhotse and Ama Dablam, helping our bodies adjust for the higher altitudes ahead.

4-5 hrs 4.5 km
Day 8

Trek to Lobuche (4,910m)

We cross the wide valley to Thukla, then climb steeply to the top of Thukla Pass — home to moving stone memorials dedicated to climbers who lost their lives on Everest. A gentle climb brings us on to Lobuche.

5-6 hrs 8 km
Day 9

Gorak Shep (5,140m) & Everest Base Camp (5,364m)

The big day. We trek over rocky glacier trails to Gorak Shep, check into our lodge, and push on to our ultimate goal — Everest Base Camp. We stand at the foot of the world's highest peak, watching the Khumbu Icefall, before returning to Gorak Shep for the night.

7-8 hrs 12 km

Dress in layers — the wind off the Khumbu Glacier is biting even on sunny spring afternoons.

Day 10

Kala Patthar Sunrise (5,550m) & Descent to Pheriche (4,240m)

We wake at 4:00 AM and climb steeply to the summit of Kala Patthar, the highest point of our trek — rewarded with the ultimate golden sunrise over Everest, Nuptse and Lhotse. After breakfast in Gorak Shep, we descend all the way to Pheriche.

6-7 hrs 12 km
Day 11

Trek back to Namche Bazaar (3,440m)

We descend through Pangboche and Tengboche, cross the suspension bridge and climb back up to Namche Bazaar. Walking feels much easier today as we drop into thicker air.

7-8 hrs 19 km
Day 12

Trek to Lukla (2,840m)

On our last trekking day we descend from Namche back to the Dudh Koshi River and continue via Monjo to Lukla. In the evening, we celebrate the completion of this legendary journey with our guides and porters.

6-7 hrs 18.5 km
Day 13

Fly Lukla to Kathmandu

We catch an early morning 35-minute flight from Lukla back to Kathmandu and transfer to our hotel. In the evening, we gather for a farewell dinner in Thamel.

Day 14

Final Departure

Transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport according to your flight schedule. Depart with unforgettable memories of the Khumbu Himalaya.

Frequently Asked Questions

The EBC trek is rated hard. You will walk 5-8 hours a day for 12 days and sleep above 4,000m for several nights. No technical climbing is involved, but good cardiovascular fitness and prior multi-day hiking experience are strongly recommended — start training with runs, stair climbs or long hikes at least two months before departure.
Spring (March-May) and autumn (late September-November) offer the most stable weather and clearest mountain views. Autumn skies are slightly clearer; spring adds blooming rhododendrons lower down and the buzz of Everest expedition teams at base camp.
Flights in and out of Lukla are highly weather-dependent and can be delayed or cancelled at short notice. We monitor conditions closely, rebook you on the next available flight and can arrange helicopter alternatives at extra cost. We strongly advise keeping 1-2 buffer days in Kathmandu before your international departure.
Our 14-day itinerary includes two dedicated acclimatization days in Namche Bazaar and Dingboche, gradual daily elevation gains, and guides trained to recognize AMS symptoms early. Staying well hydrated, walking at a slow and steady pace and honest communication with your guide are the keys to a safe ascent.
