The Journey

Trek Overview & Route

The Manaslu Circuit trek is one of Nepal's most beautiful and least-crowded off-the-beaten-path adventures, circumnavigating Mt. Manaslu (8,163m) — the world's eighth highest peak. As a government-designated restricted area, it requires a special permit and a licensed guide, which keeps the trails blissfully quiet. The journey follows the dramatic Budhi Gandaki River gorge into a high-Himalayan realm where the Nubri and Tsum communities preserve genuine Tibetan-influenced Buddhist culture, before culminating in the thrilling crossing of Larkya La Pass (5,165m) and the descent into the lush Bimtang valley.

  • Route: Machha Khola → Jagat → Sama Gaon → Larkya La (5,165m) → Bimtang → Tilije
  • Permits: restricted-area rules require min. two trekkers and a licensed guide — we handle it all
  • Difficulty: hard — long days and serious altitude, rewarded with true wilderness

Day by Day

Your 14-Day Manaslu Itinerary

From subtropical river gorge to a glaciated 5,165-meter pass — a complete circuit of the world's eighth highest mountain.

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400m)

Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be met and transferred to your hotel in Thamel. Welcome briefing with your guide and permit preparation in the evening.

Day 2

Kathmandu Sightseeing & Trek Preparation

A free day in Kathmandu — visit Pashupatinath Temple, Boudhanath Stupa and Swayambhunath. The day is also used for finalizing your restricted-area permits, equipment checks and completing any remaining preparations.

Bring your original passport today — the Restricted Area Permit cannot be issued without it.

Day 3

Drive to Machha Khola (900m)

A long drive takes us from Kathmandu through Arughat to Machha Khola, following the Budhi Gandaki River. The road winds through villages and riverside terrain to our first overnight stop on the circuit.

7-8 hrs drive
Day 4

Trek to Jagat (1,340m)

We begin our trek, climbing through forest and crossing suspension bridges over the Budhi Gandaki. We pass the natural hot springs at Tatopani and reach Jagat — the checkpoint and gateway to the Manaslu Conservation Area.

6-7 hrs
Day 5

Trek to Deng (1,865m)

The trail crosses and re-crosses the Budhi Gandaki through forests and past small settlements. We reach Deng, where the influence of Tibetan culture begins to emerge with mani walls and prayer flags.

6-7 hrs
Day 6

Trek to Namrung (2,630m)

We continue climbing through rhododendron and pine forests, crossing the river and ascending to the beautiful Buddhist village of Namrung — where chortens, mani walls and prayer flags mark the beginning of authentic high-Himalayan culture.

5-6 hrs
Day 7

Trek to Sama Gaon (3,520m)

Today's trail offers dramatic, close-up views of Mt. Manaslu and surrounding peaks. Passing through the village of Lho, we arrive at Sama Gaon — the largest high-altitude settlement on the circuit, with a spectacular backdrop of Manaslu.

7-8 hrs
Day 8

Acclimatization Day in Sama Gaon

A crucial acclimatization day. For an optional morning hike, we can trek toward Manaslu Base Camp (4,400m) or visit the sacred Birendra Lake for outstanding views of the glacier and the mountain, before resting in Sama Gaon for the night.

The Birendra Lake walk is gentler — save your legs if you're feeling the altitude.

Day 9

Trek to Samado (3,875m)

We make a short but steep climb to Samado, a small village close to the Tibetan border where snow leopards and wild blue sheep are occasionally spotted. The landscape grows dramatically more barren and high-alpine.

3-4 hrs
Day 10

Trek to Dharamsala via Larkya Base Camp (4,460m)

We climb past the Larkya Glacier base camp to reach Dharamsala (Larkya Phedi), the last tea house before the pass. The path is short but steep and dry — we rest early tonight to prepare for tomorrow's long crossing.

4-5 hrs
Day 11

Cross Larkya La Pass (5,165m) & Descend to Bimtang (3,590m)

The most important and challenging day of the journey. Starting around 4:00 AM, we climb steeply for 4-5 hours across glaciated terrain to Larkya La Pass, where a breathtaking panorama of Himalayan giants awaits. After celebrating, we make the long descent to the lush green valley of Bimtang.

8-9 hrs

Headlamp, insulated gloves and windproof layers are essential — the pre-dawn wind on the pass is fierce.

Day 12

Trek to Tilije (2,300m)

We descend through forests along the Dudh Khola river, passing the villages of Goa and Tilche, arriving at Tilije — where the Manaslu Circuit meets the Annapurna Circuit route. We celebrate the completion of the circuit with our guides and porters.

6-7 hrs
Day 13

Drive back to Kathmandu

A long drive takes us from Tilije back to Kathmandu along the Marsyangdi valley. In the evening, we enjoy a traditional Nepalese farewell dinner in Thamel.

8-9 hrs drive
Day 14

Final Departure

Transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport according to your flight schedule. Depart with lasting memories of Nepal's most remote and spectacular mountain circuit.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Manaslu region is a government-designated restricted area near the Tibetan border. Solo trekking is not permitted — a minimum of two foreign trekkers with a government-licensed guide is mandatory, and the Restricted Area Permit requires your original passport. We arrange the entire permit process for you before the trek begins.
This is a hard trek with long daily walks and significant altitude gain, culminating in the 8-9 hour crossing of Larkya La Pass (5,165m). Good cardiovascular fitness is essential. Trekkers with pre-existing heart or respiratory conditions should consult a doctor before booking.
Spring (March-May) and autumn (October-November) offer stable weather and the safest conditions on Larkya La. The pass can be snowbound in winter, and monsoon landslides affect the Budhi Gandaki gorge sections in summer.
Manaslu sees a fraction of the trekkers of Everest or Annapurna. The restricted-area status preserves genuine Tibetan-influenced Nubri and Tsum culture, and the trail feels wonderfully remote while still offering tea house accommodation every night — the best of wilderness and comfort.
Yes. A valid travel insurance policy specifying high-altitude helicopter rescue is strictly required prior to booking, as emergency evacuation may be necessary in remote sections of the route where no road access exists.
