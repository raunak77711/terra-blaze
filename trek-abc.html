The Journey

Trek Overview & Route

The Annapurna Base Camp trek is one of the most popular and breathtaking trekking journeys in Nepal. This trek leads you to the south face of Mt. Annapurna I (8,091m), the 10th highest peak in the world. The trail winds through dense rhododendron forests and traditional Gurung villages, with stunning views of Machapuchare (Fishtail, 6,993m) and Annapurna South (7,219m) along the way. Standing in the natural amphitheater of the Annapurna Sanctuary, surrounded by towering snow-capped giants, is the ultimate highlight of this adventure.

  • Route: Pokhara → Ghandruk → Chhomrong → ABC (4,130m) → Jhinu Danda hot springs
  • Difficulty: moderate — 5-7 hours of walking per day on well-established trails
  • Perfect for: first-time Himalayan trekkers who want big mountain views in under two weeks

Day by Day

Your 11-Day Sanctuary Itinerary

A steadily rising trail through forests and villages into the heart of the Annapurna Sanctuary — with hot springs waiting on the way down.

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400m)

Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be met and transferred to your hotel in Thamel. The rest of the day is spent on trek preparation, equipment checks and a welcome briefing with your guide.

Day 2

Fly to Pokhara (800m) & Trek to Ghandruk (1,940m)

A scenic 25-minute flight brings us to Pokhara, followed by a 2.5-hour drive to Kimchi where our trek begins. We hike up to the famous Gurung village of Ghandruk, with spectacular views of Annapurna South and Machapuchare.

2 hrs 2 km

Ghandruk's stone terraces catch the evening light beautifully — keep your camera handy at sunset.

Day 3

Trek to Chhomrong (2,170m)

We descend from Ghandruk through forests and cross the Kimrong Khola. After a steep climb, we reach the beautiful village of Chhomrong — one of the largest settlements and hubs on the Annapurna Sanctuary route.

Day 4

Trek to Bamboo (2,310m)

We descend stone steps to the Chhomrong Khola, cross the bridge and climb to Sinuva village. From there the trail passes through dense bamboo and rhododendron forests, descending steadily to the aptly named Bamboo camp.

5-6 hrs 7 km
Day 5

Trek to Deurali (3,230m)

From Bamboo, the trail climbs steadily through alpine forests past Dovan and Himalaya camp into the high-altitude zone. As the Modi Khola valley narrows, we reach Deurali — the gateway to the upper sanctuary.

4-5 hrs 8.5 km
Day 6

Trek to Annapurna Base Camp (4,130m)

The highlight of the journey. Walking steadily from Deurali, we first reach Machapuchare Base Camp (3,700m). Another two hours of moderate climbing brings us into the Annapurna Sanctuary itself — a colossal wall of 360-degree snow peaks.

Sleeping at base camp means sunrise at base camp — the dawn light on Annapurna I is unforgettable.

Day 7

Sanctuary Sunrise & Descent to Bamboo (2,310m)

After witnessing a spectacular sunrise over the peaks, we begin our descent along the same trail, heading back down through Deurali and Dovan to Bamboo. The walk is much faster and easier as we go downhill.

Day 8

Trek to Jhinu Danda (1,760m) — Natural Hot Springs

We trek from Bamboo back up to Sinuva and Chhomrong, then descend to Jhinu Danda. Here you can relax and soothe your sore muscles in the famous natural hot springs on the banks of the Modi Khola.

5-6 hrs 9 km

Pack a swimsuit — the hot spring pools sit right beside the river, a short walk below the village.

Day 9

Trek to Matque (1,070m) & Drive to Pokhara

A short trek from Jhinu Danda brings us down to Matque, where we meet our transport for the 2.5-hour drive back to Pokhara. Enjoy a comfortable evening and celebratory dinner by the lakeside.

1-2 hrs trek
Day 10

Drive / Fly Pokhara to Kathmandu

Travel back to Kathmandu by tourist bus or a short flight. In the evening, we gather for a farewell dinner in Thamel featuring traditional Nepalese cuisine.

Day 11

International Departure

Transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport according to your flight schedule. Depart with beautiful memories of the Annapurna Sanctuary.
