The Journey

Trek Overview & Route

The Langtang Valley trek is the closest alpine trekking journey from Kathmandu. The trail leads through the dense oak and rhododendron forests of Langtang National Park into a pristine glacier valley inhabited by Tamang and Bhotia communities. Since its reconstruction after the 2015 earthquake, the region boasts well-equipped, welcoming tea houses. Highlights include the spiritual Kyanjin Gompa (3,830m), the historic yak cheese factory, and a climb up Tserko Ri (4,984m) for stunning 360-degree vistas of Langtang Lirung (7,227m) and other snow-capped giants.

  • Route: Syabrubesi → Lama Hotel → Langtang Village → Kyanjin Gompa → Tserko Ri (4,984m)
  • Access: no domestic flights — just a scenic drive from Kathmandu to the trailhead
  • Difficulty: easy to moderate — the perfect first Himalayan trek, with an optional summit day
Trek Highlights

Three Signature Langtang Experiences

Every stage of the trail is led by licensed local guides through Langtang National Park — Nepal's first Himalayan national park and a stronghold of red pandas and Tamang culture.

4,984 m

The crowning viewpoint of the valley — a challenging day-climb rewarded with one of the great panoramas of the Himalaya.

  • Unobstructed views of over 20 Himalayan peaks

  • Sunrise light sweeping across the glacier valley

Day by Day

Your 11-Day Langtang Itinerary

A gentle ascent through forest and pasture to the glacier head of the valley — capped by the optional Tserko Ri summit at dawn.

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu (1,400m)

Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, you will be met and transferred to your hotel in Thamel. The rest of the day is spent on trek preparation, equipment checks, permit processing and a welcome briefing with your guide.

Day 2

Drive to Syabrubesi (1,500m)

We drive from Kathmandu to Syabrubesi in Rasuwa district by tourist bus or private jeep. The route winds through hillsides and follows the Trishuli River, offering beautiful mountain vistas along the way.

6-7 hrs drive
Day 3

Trek to Lama Hotel (2,480m)

Our trek begins. We cross the Bhote Koshi River and hike along the banks of the Langtang Khola. The trail climbs through dense forests of oak, maple and bamboo — prime red panda habitat — to reach Lama Hotel.

6-7 hrs
Day 4

Trek to Langtang Village (3,430m)

We continue climbing through forests, emerging at Ghodatabela where there is a national park checkpoint. The valley widens as we pass yak pastures and traditional stone houses, arriving at the beautifully rebuilt Langtang Village.

5-6 hrs
Day 5

Trek to Kyanjin Gompa (3,830m)

The trail ascends gently through yak pastures and small villages. We pass large mani walls and chortens to arrive at Kyanjin Gompa, surrounded by massive peaks. Here we can visit the monastery and the famous local yak cheese factory.

2-3 hrs

Arriving early leaves the whole afternoon for the bakery, the cheese factory and short glacier viewpoints.

Day 6

Climb Tserko Ri (4,984m)

Today we embark on a challenging acclimatization climb to Tserko Ri, the highest viewpoint of our trek. The effort is rewarded with breathtaking, unobstructed views of over 20 Himalayan peaks and glaciers, including Langtang Lirung (7,227m). We return to Kyanjin Gompa for the night.

7-8 hrs

Start before dawn — the summit panorama is clearest in the still morning air.

Day 7

Trek back to Lama Hotel (2,480m)

We begin our return journey, descending along the same trail through Langtang Village and Ghodatabela, arriving back at Lama Hotel. The downhill walk is much faster and easier.

6-7 hrs
Day 8

Trek to Thulo Syabru (2,210m)

From Lama Hotel, we descend through forest and then climb to the charming traditional village of Thulo Syabru, which offers lovely views of the surrounding hills and the valley below.

5-6 hrs
Day 9

Trek to Dhunche (1,960m)

We descend from Thulo Syabru to the district headquarters of Dhunche. In the evening, we celebrate the successful completion of our trek with our guide and porters.

3-4 hrs
Day 10

Drive back to Kathmandu (1,400m)

We take a bus or private jeep from Dhunche back to Kathmandu and check into the hotel. In the evening, we gather for a farewell dinner in Thamel.

5-6 hrs drive
Day 11

Final Departure

Transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport for your flight home. Depart with beautiful memories of the Langtang Valley.

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Langtang is one of Nepal's gentler alpine treks, rated easy to moderate. Daily walks are 3-7 hours on good trails. The optional climb of Tserko Ri (4,984m) is the one strenuous day and requires good stamina and conditioning — but it can be skipped or swapped for the shorter Kyanjin Ri viewpoint.
Langtang is the closest trekking region to Kathmandu — no weather-dependent domestic flights, just a scenic drive to Syabrubesi. You get glaciers, 7,000-meter peaks and authentic Tamang culture in a far quieter valley, all in under two weeks.
Tea houses provide basic beds and blankets, but we highly recommend carrying your own -10°C rated sleeping bag for comfort and hygiene — especially from November to February, when nights at Kyanjin Gompa drop well below freezing.
Spring (March-May) brings blooming rhododendron forests in the lower valley, while autumn (October-November) offers the clearest skies for the Tserko Ri panorama. Both seasons have stable weather and comfortable trekking temperatures.
Yes. Travel insurance covering high-altitude helicopter evacuation is mandatory. Although Langtang is gentler than other routes, the upper valley sits above 3,800 meters, where emergency rescue is only possible by air.
