Package Highlights

Five Signature Jungle Experiences

Every activity is led by licensed naturalist guides inside and around Chitwan National Park — a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home of the one-horned rhinoceros and the Royal Bengal tiger.

2.5 Hours

Jeep Safari

Ride deep into the core zone of Chitwan National Park in an open 4x4 with an expert wildlife spotter on board.

  • Explore the grasslands and sal forests of the park
  • Chance to see rhinos, deer, crocodiles and exotic birds
  • If luck favors — the elusive Royal Bengal tiger
Traditional

Elephant Ride

A classic Chitwan experience — sway gently above the tall elephant grass on a guided safari through the community forest.

  • Traditional jungle experience led by expert mahouts
  • Guided safari through forest and grassland areas
  • Wildlife often approaches closer than to vehicles
Rapti River

Canoe Ride

Drift silently down the Rapti River in a traditional dugout canoe carved from a single kapok tree trunk.

  • Peaceful float along the Rapti River at water level
  • Spot gharial and marsh mugger crocodiles basking
  • Kingfishers, storks and over 500 recorded bird species
Culture & Nature

Breeding Center & Tharu Village

Meet playful elephant calves at the government-run breeding center, then wander a living Tharu village.

  • Visit the Elephant Breeding Center at Khorsor
  • Explore traditional mud-and-thatch Tharu homes
  • Learn the lifestyle of Chitwan's indigenous people
Evening Show

Tharu Cultural Program

As dusk falls, the rhythm of drums fills the air — an unforgettable evening of indigenous performance art.

  • Evening cultural performance at the community hall
  • Famous Tharu stick dance and peacock dance
  • Traditional music, costumes and audience participation
Day by Day

Your 3-Day Jungle Itinerary

A perfectly paced blend of wildlife, river, culture and relaxation — crafted so you never feel rushed, yet never miss a moment of the jungle.

Day 1

Arrival & Cultural Experience

  • Midday: Arrival in Sauraha, Chitwan — greeted with a refreshing welcome drink and resort check-in
  • 13:00: Lunch at the resort overlooking the gardens
  • 16:00: Guided walk through a traditional Tharu village and stroll to the Rapti riverbank for sunset
  • 19:00: Evening Tharu cultural dance program — stick dance, peacock dance and folk music
  • 20:30: Dinner and overnight stay at the resort

Tip: arrive by early afternoon to enjoy the full village walk and riverside sunset.

Day 2

Full Jungle Adventure Day

  • 06:30: Wake-up call with the jungle chorus, followed by breakfast
  • 08:00: Jeep Safari (approx. 2.5 hours) into Chitwan National Park — rhinos, deer, crocodiles, wild boar and abundant birdlife
  • 12:30: Lunch back at the resort
  • 14:30: Elephant ride through the community forest — a traditional guided jungle experience
  • 16:30: Dugout canoe ride on the Rapti River, drifting past basking crocodiles and aquatic birds
  • 18:00: Sunset viewing over the river — Chitwan's most photographed moment
  • 19:30: Dinner and overnight stay at the resort

Wear neutral colors and bring binoculars — early morning is prime rhino-sighting time.

Day 3

Nature & Departure

  • 07:00: Early breakfast at the resort
  • 08:30: Visit the Elephant Breeding Center at Khorsor — meet the calves and learn about conservation
  • 10:30: Free time for photography, birdwatching or simply relaxing in the resort gardens
  • 12:00: Farewell lunch
  • 13:30: Check-out and departure with a heart full of jungle memories

We can arrange onward tourist bus or flight connections to Kathmandu, Pokhara or Lumbini.

October to March brings cool, dry, pleasant weather. February to May is the prime wildlife-viewing window — the tall elephant grass is freshly cut, so rhinos, deer and even tigers are far easier to spot near the water sources.
Sauraha is a comfortable 5–6 hour tourist bus ride from either Kathmandu or Pokhara, or a scenic 20-minute flight to Bharatpur Airport followed by a 30-minute drive. Transport isn't included in the package price, but we're happy to arrange bus tickets or flights for you — just mention it in your booking request.
Chitwan has one of the highest densities of greater one-horned rhinos in the world — over 690 at the last count — so sightings are very common. That said, these are wild animals and nothing is ever guaranteed. Our naturalist guides know the park's habits intimately and will maximize your chances.
