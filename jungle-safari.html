About the Park

One of Asia's Great Wildlife Sanctuaries

Established in 1973 as Nepal's very first national park and declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984, Chitwan protects one of the last great expanses of Terai lowland jungle, sal forest and riverine grassland in South Asia.

Along the braided channels of the Rapti and Narayani rivers, this is genuinely one of the finest wildlife-viewing destinations on Earth outside Africa — and your safari puts you right in the middle of it, by jeep, by canoe and on foot.

Safari Highlights

Six Signature Jungle Experiences

Every activity is led by licensed naturalist guides inside and around Chitwan National Park — designed around genuine, low-impact wildlife encounters.

Morning

Jeep Safari

Ride deep into the core zone of Chitwan National Park in an open 4x4 with an expert wildlife spotter on board.

  • Grasslands and sal forest at their most alive
  • Rhinos, deer, crocodiles and exotic birds
  • If luck favors — the elusive Royal Bengal tiger
Sunrise

Canoe Ride on the Rapti

Drift silently down the Rapti River in a traditional dugout canoe carved from a single kapok trunk.

  • Peaceful float along the river at water level
  • Spot gharial and marsh mugger crocodiles basking
  • Kingfishers, storks and dozens of waterbirds
On Foot

Guided Walk & Birdwatching

A slower, quieter way to read the jungle — tracking prints, calls and over 500 recorded bird species with a trained naturalist.

  • Community forest trails away from vehicle tracks
  • Kingfishers, hornbills, storks and eagles
  • Learn to read tracks, calls and warning signs
Ethical

Elephant Conservation Encounter

No elephant-back riding — instead, meet rescued and conservation elephants at the Khorsor Breeding Centre and learn how they're cared for.

  • Meet playful calves and their mahout caretakers
  • Learn about Chitwan's elephant conservation work
  • A responsible, low-impact wildlife experience
Culture

Tharu Village Walk

Wander a living Tharu village of mud-and-thatch homes and meet Chitwan's indigenous community on their own ground.

  • Traditional mud-and-thatch Tharu architecture
  • Learn the daily lifestyle of the indigenous Tharu
  • Ends at the Rapti riverbank for sunset
Evening

Tharu Cultural Dance

As dusk falls, the rhythm of drums fills the air — an unforgettable evening of indigenous performance art.

  • Live performance at the community hall
  • Famous Tharu stick dance and peacock dance
  • Traditional music, costume and audience participation
Field Guide

Wildlife You May Encounter

Chitwan's core zone and buffer forests hold one of Asia's richest concentrations of wildlife. Here's who you might meet along the way.

Frequent R

One-Horned Rhinoceros

Gainda

Chitwan is home to over 700 greater one-horned rhinos — one of the highest densities on Earth. Most sightings happen on the morning jeep safari.

Elusive T

Royal Bengal Tiger

Pate Bagh

One of Nepal's last tiger strongholds. A sighting is rare and unforgettable — patient guides know exactly where to look.

Seasonal E

Wild Asian Elephant

Hatti

Wild herds roam the park's forests, while rescued and orphaned elephants are cared for at the Khorsor Breeding Centre.

Common C

Marsh Mugger & Gharial

Gohi

Watch both crocodile species bask on the Rapti's sandbanks during your sunrise canoe ride.

Abundant D

Spotted Deer

Chital Mriga

Nepal's most graceful deer moves in large herds through the sal forest and grasslands — a near-guaranteed sighting.

Common M

Rhesus Macaque

Bandar

Playful troops swing through riverside trees, often announcing bigger wildlife nearby with their alarm calls.

500+ Species B

Exotic Birds

Chara

Kingfishers, hornbills, storks and eagles — Chitwan is one of Asia's richest birdwatching destinations.
