Essential Packing List for Annapurna Treks

By Sunita Tamang · June 28, 2026 · 6 Min Read

Gear & Equipment • June 28, 2026 • 6 Min Read

Whether you're doing the Annapurna Base Camp trek (7 days) or the full Annapurna Circuit (15+ days), what you carry can make or break your journey. This is the definitive gear checklist from our team.

Golden Rules
  • Your pack should not exceed 8–10 kg (excluding water)
  • Every item should have a purpose — cut ruthlessly
  • Hire a porter for anything over 10 kg; don't risk your knees

Clothing — The Layer System

Temperature swings of 25°C between valley and high camp are normal in the Annapurna region. The layering system is not optional — it is survival strategy.

Base Layer (moisture-wicking)

  • ✓ 2× merino wool or synthetic long-sleeve shirts
  • ✓ 2× thermal underwear bottoms
  • ✓ 4× trekking socks (wool blend)
  • ✓ 3× moisture-wicking underwear

Mid Layer (insulation)

  • ✓ 1× fleece jacket or mid-layer pullover
  • ✓ 1× insulated down jacket (critical above 3,500 m)
  • ✓ 1× trekking trousers (zip-off convertible recommended)

Outer Shell (wind & rain)

  • ✓ 1× waterproof hardshell jacket (Gore-Tex or equivalent)
  • ✓ 1× waterproof trousers

Accessories

  • ✓ Warm beanie / wool hat
  • ✓ Sun hat or wide-brim cap
  • ✓ Neck gaiter / buff
  • ✓ Lightweight gloves + warm insulated gloves
  • ✓ UV-protective sunglasses (category 4)

Footwear

Your boots are your single most important piece of gear. Blisters on Day 2 can end a trek that took months to plan.

  • ✓ Waterproof trekking boots — ankle support, worn-in before departure
  • ✓ Lightweight camp sandals or Crocs (for teahouse evenings)
  • ✓ Gaiters (for snow sections above 4,000 m in winter/spring)
  • ✓ Blister prevention (Bodyglide or moleskin padding)
Boot Tip: Wear your trekking boots for at least 3–4 full days of hiking before your Nepal trip. Never arrive with brand-new boots.

Sleeping Bag & Gear

  • ✓ Sleeping bag — rated to −10°C for high camps; teahouses provide blankets but not quilts at altitude
  • ✓ Trekking poles (highly recommended, especially on descents)
  • ✓ Daypack 25–30L with rain cover
  • ✓ Main backpack or duffel for porter (50–60L)
  • ✓ Headlamp + spare batteries (power cuts are common)
  • ✓ Dry bags / zip-lock bags for electronics

Medical Kit

Important: Carry a personal medical kit regardless of whether you have a guide. Pharmacies are limited above Pokhara.
  • ✓ Diamox (acetazolamide) for AMS prevention — consult your doctor
  • ✓ Ibuprofen & paracetamol
  • ✓ Diarrhea medication (Imodium, oral rehydration salts)
  • ✓ Broad-spectrum antibiotics (doctor's prescription)
  • ✓ Throat lozenges & antihistamines
  • ✓ Bandages, antiseptic wipes, blister patches
  • ✓ SPF 50+ sunscreen + lip balm with SPF
  • ✓ Water purification tablets or Steripen UV purifier

Electronics & Documents

  • ✓ Passport + photocopies (keep one in your daypack)
  • ✓ TIMS card + ACAP / ANPAP permit
  • ✓ Travel insurance documents (must include helicopter evacuation)
  • ✓ Power bank (10,000 mAh minimum)
  • ✓ Universal adapter + charging cables
  • ✓ Offline maps downloaded (Maps.me or Gaia GPS)
  • ✓ Camera with extra memory cards

What NOT to Bring

Overpacking is one of the most common trekking mistakes. Leave these at home or store them at your Pokhara hotel:

  • Jeans or cotton trousers (heavy, slow to dry)
  • Excessive toiletries — teahouses have basic supplies
  • Multiple pairs of shoes
  • Laptop (use your phone; weight matters at altitude)
