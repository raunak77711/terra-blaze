Manaslu Circuit: The Complete Trekker's Guide to Nepal's Hidden Gem

By Rajesh Shrestha · June 15, 2026 · 8 Min Read

Trekking Guide • June 15, 2026 • 8 Min Read

While crowds queue for Everest Base Camp and Annapurna Base Camp, a spectacular circuit around the world's eighth-highest mountain remains gloriously, almost secretively, uncrowded. The Manaslu Circuit is Nepal's finest hidden gem — and this guide tells you everything you need to know.

Manaslu at a Glance
  • Distance: ~177 km circuit
  • Duration: 14–18 days
  • Max altitude: Larkya La Pass, 5,160 m
  • Difficulty: Challenging (some high-altitude experience recommended)
  • Best season: Autumn (Oct–Nov) and Spring (Mar–May)

Why the Manaslu Circuit?

Opened to foreign trekkers only in 1992, the Manaslu Conservation Area still has the authentic wilderness character the Annapurna Circuit had thirty years ago — before jeep roads and teahouse hotels changed its texture. Here, the trails feel genuinely remote. Porters carry loads in dokos (traditional wicker baskets). Villages of stone-walled houses and spinning prayer wheels dot the hillsides. Yak caravans block the trail at unexpected moments.

The route circumnavigates Mt. Manaslu (8,163 m), the world's eighth-highest peak, offering consistently dramatic views throughout the trek — not just at a single base camp endpoint.

The Route Overview

The circuit begins and ends in the Tsum Valley gateway town of Soti Khola (710 m), accessed from Kathmandu via a 7–8 hour jeep or bus ride. From there, the trail climbs the Buri Gandaki gorge — one of the deepest river canyons in Nepal — before ascending into the high-altitude Nubri and Tsum regions, ethnically and culturally Tibetan.

The dramatic high point is the Larkya La Pass (5,160 m), crossed on approximately Day 12–13 of a standard itinerary. The crossing is a long, demanding day (8–10 hours) with significant snow and ice from November onwards. From the pass, the trail descends steeply to Bhimthang and then out to the roadhead at Dharapani on the old Annapurna Circuit.

Permits Required

The Manaslu region is a Restricted Area, which means the permit requirements and costs are significantly higher than standard trekking areas:

  • ✓ Restricted Area Permit (RAP): USD 100/week Sept–Nov (peak season); USD 75/week Dec–Aug — mandatory, issued in Kathmandu
  • ✓ Manaslu Conservation Area Project (MCAP) permit: NPR 3,000
  • ✓ Annapurna Conservation Area Permit (ACAP): NPR 3,000 (for the Dharapani exit)
  • ✓ TIMS card
  • ! A licensed guide is mandatory on the Manaslu Circuit — solo trekking is not permitted

Difficulty & Fitness

The Manaslu Circuit is rated challenging. The long, physically demanding days leading up to and including the Larkya La crossing, combined with sustained altitude above 4,000 m for several days, mean this trek is better suited to those with some previous high-altitude experience (e.g., Langtang, Annapurna Base Camp).

That said, it is not a technical mountaineering objective — the Larkya La requires only basic snow-crossing skills and a slow, steady pace. Good cardiovascular fitness and proper acclimatisation will get you there.

Trekker Tip: Start your Larkya La crossing day by headlamp (4:00–4:30 AM) to reach the summit before afternoon clouds and cold winds build. The sunrise from the pass over Himalchuli and the Ganesh Himal is unforgettable.

Best Season

Autumn (October–November) is the prime season: stable weather, clear skies, and good snow conditions on the pass. Spring (late March–May) is equally good for weather and offers the bonus of rhododendron blooms at lower altitudes.

Avoid the monsoon (June–September) when the Buri Gandaki gorge is prone to landslides and the trail becomes extremely muddy and leech-infested.
